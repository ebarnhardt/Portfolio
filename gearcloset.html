Gear Closet

Add the gear you own to your electronic gear closet to find gaps in what you own or the best replacements.

Idea generation, UX documentation, user research, wireframing,
onboarding design, UI design

The Challenge

Personalized Gear Advice

Skiers, rock climbers, surfers, cyclists, paddlers and other outdoor sport athletes are serious about their gear. Quality gear might add comfort, performance or safety elements to a sport these athletes do frequently. The best gear allows them to perform at their highest level, allowing opportunities for improvement and achievement as well as having the most fun. Sites like Backcountry have become popular outdoor retailers because of their community of consumers that give each other advice on the best product to buy. The challenge for Gear Closet is to take these reviews and recommendations one step farther by offering personalized recommendations on which gear to buy based on what a user already owns.
Research + Discovery

Outdoor Athletes

I created six personas based around different user goals for an e-commerce site - browsing products,viewing details about an item, adding products to a shopping cart, paying for items, signing up for an account and submitting reviews for a product.
After the wireframing stage, I adjusted the onbarding designs to place mroe focus on the gear diagram. Instead of so many drop-downs and other features around the diagram, I moved as much functionality into the diagram itself.
Onboarding Design

Keeping the User Engaged

All of the information that Gear Closet uses to offer personalized gear recommendations to users is gathered during the onboarding process. Users add the specific gear they own to a digital gear closet where they can then rate each item on a variety of attributes to find the best replacements. Additionally, beginner athletes can see which gear is recommended for each sport to help them find what they need to get started. The challenge with such a lengthy onboarding process is to keep the user engaged and prevent drop-off. The setup wizard helps keep the user on track and motivated to finish. Additionally, on each screen the user is given the option to save the return to filling out their closet later.
I created a list of user stories that helped me determine which pages and features I needed to include in the design. I then developed a series of user flows and figured out how the personas would move through the site.
I tested the Invision protoype with some outdoor atheletes and confirmed my suspicions that there is some interest in this kind of product. Testers liked the idea of the Gear Closet community where they could look up other friends’ (or even pro athletes’) closets to see how they like their gear. This could be the solution outdoor athletes are looking for to get the best gear available.
